A Quick Guide To Custom Keyboards

Introduction

Custom keyboards have been a very niche hobby for the past couple of years but is finally garnering some attention in the mainstream market, meaning that many consumers are new to the whole hobby without having the slightest idea of what to do when trying to build their own custom keyboard. This task can be very daunting, but with the help of this keyboard guide website, you will know everything you need to know within minutes.

Parts

These are the different parts of a keyboard:

  • Keycaps
  • Switches
  • Stabilizers
  • Plate
  • PCB
  • Case

Form Factors

This brings us to the next piece of important information you will need which is the form factor of your custom keyboard. You probably already know that there are different sizes of keyboards made by larger companies but in the keyboard community, there are many specialized layouts that can suit your needs depending on any particular constraint.

Here is a chart of the different enthusiast form factors:

Name Size % Switch #
Full-Sized 100% 108 Switches
1800 Compact 96% 96 Switches
Tenkeyless 80% 87 Switches
75 Key 75% 84 Switches
65 Key 65% 70 Switches
Poker 60% 61 Switches
40 Key 40% 45 Switches
